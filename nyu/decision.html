Dear John, On behalf of the admissions committee, it is my honor and privilege to share with you that you have been admitted to the Stern School of Business at New York University, and the NYU Class of 2028. Congratulations! In 1831, NYU was founded by Albert Gallatin, Treasury Secretary to U.S. Presidents Thomas Jefferson and James Madison, to do something that had never been done before in higher education â€” establish a university that was open to students of all backgrounds based on their talent and merit alone. In short, a University without walls. NYU has built on this boundaryless foundation to become the largest independent research university in the United States, and one of the most influential institutions of higher learning in the world. When you return to your status page, you will see a checklist of next steps for admitted students, including how to accept your offer of admission, which you must do by January 3, and how to view your financial aid award, if you applied for aid. If we do not receive your decision by the deadline, we will relinquish your space in our class. Once you confirm your enrollment, it I your ethical obligation to withdraw any applications you may have submitted to other colleges or universities. Please know that your offer of admission is contingent upon successful completion of your existing academic program at a performance level comparable to the one presented in your application. Visit the admitted student section of our website to review our expectations for admitted students. John, you should be extremely proud of your accomplishments, and I encourage you to celebrate with your family and friends who have supported you thus far on your intellectual journey. We look forward to welcoming you to the NYU community, and invite you to connect with new classmates on the NYU Class of 2028 Facebook page. Sincerely, Jonathan B. Williams Associate Vice President, Undergraduate Admissions
